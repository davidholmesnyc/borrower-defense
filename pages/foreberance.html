Section 6: No Payments while Under Review

You may request to have your federal student loan(s) placed into Forbearance For loans currently being paid, forbearance means that you do not have to make loan payments and there will be no penalty to you. or Stopped Collections For loans in default, stopped collections means that attempts to collect payment will stop and you can pause making payments. If you have loans undergoing rehabilitation or you are unsure about the status of your loans, please contact your federal student loan servicer (the company that sends you statements) status while your application is under review. Please read the "Common Questions and Answers" page before you choose to enter forbearance or stopped collections.

Note that interest will continue to accumulate on all of these federal loans, including subsidized loans. If your application for borrower defense is denied, or partially approved, the total amount you owe will be higher.

You do not have to place your loans in forbearance or stopped collections to apply for borrower defense relief.

I want all my federal loans to be placed in forbearance and for collections to stop on any loans in default while my borrower defense application is reviewed. During this time period, I understand that interest will continue to accrue.

I do not want all of my federal loans to be placed in forbearance and for collections to stop on any loans in default while my borrower defense application is reviewed. During this time period, I understand that interest will continue to accrue and that I must continue to make loan payments.
